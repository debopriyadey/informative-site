The Indian Population

The growing population

The population of India represents 17.99 percent of the world´s total population which arguably means that one person in every 6 people on the planet is a resident of India. The Worker Population Ratio in India stood at 46.8 percent in the 2017-18 fiscal year, the Periodic Labour Force Survey (PLFS) of the National Sample Survey Office (NSSO) showed. The total population in India was estimated at 1312.2 million people in 2019, according to the latest census figures. Health Metrics and Evaluation argues that while India is destined to be the largest country in the world, its population will peak by mid-century. And as the 21st century closes, its ultimate population will be far smaller than anyone could have anticipated, about 1.09 billion instead of approximately 1.35 billion today. It could even be as low as 724 million deminishing human resource..

As a Resource

The Labour Force Participation Rate measures the number of persons aged 15 and over who are employed and unemployed but looking for a job divided by the total working-age population. In India, the unemployment rate is estimated by directly interviewing a large sample of randomly selected households. Centre for Monitoring Indian Economy Consumer Pyramids panel of households includes over 174,405 households including over 522,000 members who are over 15 years old. In India, National Floor Level Minimum Wage (NFLMW) is the minimum wage below which no state government can fix the minimum wage. This is a non-statutory measure to ensure upward revision of minimum wages in different states. The central government introduced the NFLMW in 1996.

Major produced human resources in india

Medical Graduate

There are currently 370 medical colleges in the country that offer 49,840 MBBS seats between them. This makes India the largest creator of doctors in the world. In comparison, the United States only produces 18,000 doctors a year.

Engineers

India provides 1.5 million seats in various engineering colleges across the country. . roughly 7.5 lakh engineers graduate each year.Which makes india highest producer of engineering graduates annually..

MBA Graduate

Annually about 3,60,000 MBA students graduate from 4000 B-schools in India and 61% are unemployable due to skill gaps and lesser work experience. Indian MBA graduates have lost their edge and there has been a drop in their employment over the last few years.

Human Labour

Census 2011 has shown that out of about 55.5 million marginal workers seeking/available for work in India, 21.9 million are illiterate, followed by 20.9 million (37.6%) who have studied below secondary level and work as hand labours in factories and multiple sites.
